# Acme Dental Practice - Business Plan 2025

## Business Overview
Acme Dental Practice is a general dental clinic in suburban Sydney.
Owner-operated by Dr. Sarah Mitchell with 2 part-time staff.
- 1 dental hygienist (3 days/week)
- 1 receptionist (4 days/week)

Established: 2021
Location: Parramatta, NSW
Operating hours: Mon-Fri 8am-5pm

## Financial Goals for 2025

### Revenue Targets
- Annual Revenue Target: $450,000
- Monthly Revenue Target: $37,500
- Quarterly Revenue Target: $112,500

### Profit Goals
- Target Profit Margin: 25% ($112,500 annual profit)
- Monthly Profit Target: $9,375

### Cost Structure (Monthly)
- Rent & utilities: $4,500
- Staff wages: $12,000
- Supplies & consumables: $3,500
- Insurance: $800
- Marketing: $1,000
- Other overheads: $2,700
- Total Fixed Costs: ~$24,500/month

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

### Patient Metrics
- New patients per month: 15 (target)
- Patient retention rate: 85% (target)
- Active patient base: 800 (current), 1000 (target by Dec)

### Revenue Metrics
- Average treatment value: $350
- Revenue per chair hour: $180
- Chair utilization rate: 75% (target)

### Operational Metrics
- Appointment no-show rate: <10%
- Same-day treatment acceptance: 60%

## Hiring Plan

### When to Hire a Dental Assistant
We will hire a full-time dental assistant when ALL of these conditions are met:
1. Monthly revenue exceeds $40,000 for 3 consecutive months
2. Chair utilization consistently above 80%
3. Waitlist for appointments exceeds 2 weeks

### Budget for New Hire
- Salary: $50,000/year
- Superannuation: $5,500/year (11%)
- Total cost: $55,000/year ($4,583/month)
- Break-even requirement: Additional $5,000/month revenue to cover cost

### Impact Assessment
A dental assistant would:
- Increase chair time efficiency by 20%
- Allow Dr. Mitchell to see 2 more patients/day
- Potential additional revenue: $8,000-10,000/month

## Cash Flow Requirements

### Minimum Operating Cash
- Required buffer: $30,000 (covers ~5 weeks of expenses)
- Current target: Maintain $35,000-50,000 in operating account

### Reserve Funds
- Equipment replacement fund: $5,000/quarter ($20,000/year)
- Emergency fund: $25,000 (6 months of minimal operations)

### Major Upcoming Expenses
- New dental chair (Q3 2025): $15,000
- Digital X-ray upgrade (Q4 2025): $12,000
- Office renovation (2026): $40,000

## Growth Strategy

### Q1-Q2 2025: Consolidation
- Focus on patient retention
- Optimize scheduling efficiency
- Build cash reserves

### Q3 2025: Expansion Preparation
- If revenue targets met: hire dental assistant
- Extend to Saturday hours (if demand supports)
- Upgrade digital X-ray

### Q4 2025: Service Expansion
- Add orthodontic services (Invisalign)
- Partner with local specialists for referrals
- Target: 20% revenue growth year-over-year

## Risk Factors

### Financial Risks
- Economic downturn reducing discretionary dental spending
- Rising supply costs (materials up 15% in 2024)
- Staff turnover/sick leave

### Mitigation Strategies
- Maintain 3+ months cash buffer
- Diversify services (cosmetic, orthodontics)
- Competitive staff compensation
